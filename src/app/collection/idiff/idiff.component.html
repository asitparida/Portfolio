iDiff

A visual tool to compare multiple fidelities of designs

Why

did I build it ?

The users would be able to compare interactions, levels of visual progress and delta changes in different screens. The tool could also be used to communicate the intent of the designers and project a design evolution to be used in portfolios.

What

are the use-cases ?

I combined the click-through prototyping features (e.g. Invision) for visualizing multiple levels of prototypes at the same time. Meaning we would be able to compare lo-fi, mid-fi and hi-fi prototypes all together.

Who

is my audience ?

The target audience are primarily designers working in visual design space. Folks here nominally create multiple fidelities for designs. This tool would be helpful for them to allow click-through prototyping while comparing two designs or screens side-by-side.

Ideation

The challenge was to create a web-based tool for aiding fellow designers and HCI students in my cohort at university. Just two weeks earlier, I was creating paper-prototypes for my very first classroom project. I was fascinated by the design evolution between iterations.
So, I thought of somehow combining the most enjoyable parts of projects. After hours of brainstorming, I finally decided build a collborative showcase tool for UX practitioners to visualize lo-fi and hi-fi resolution of designs we work on. These users can showcase their multiple screens on a split-screen like display.
In addition, the tool would have to extend the current click-through capabilities for existing services like Invision.

Paper Prototyping

I created paper prototypes after initial ideation for a quick validation of the project idea.
I started sketching out both the authoring and the previewing views. I chose to focus on having a coherent stack of controls which would employ ample affordance. And so, the drag-bar handler was quite effective for feedforward.

Tenet #1

For authoring view, move controls to a single pane, either to left or right, to minmise jumping around the screens.

Tenet #2

For previewing, look to enable both horizontal and vertical splits for comparing fidelities.

Tenet #3

Add mutliple screen/device masks to enhance preview experience.
