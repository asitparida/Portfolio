iDiff

A visual tool to compare multiple fidelities of designs

View Prototype

Why

did I build it ?

The users would be able to compare interactions, levels of visible progress and delta changes in different screens. The tool could also be used to communicate the intent of the designers and and project the evolution of designs. The tool can be quite handy for portfolios.

What

are the use-cases ?

I combined the click-through prototyping features (e.g. Invision) for visualizing multiple levels of prototypes at the same time. Meaning we would be able to compare lo-fi, mid-fi and hi-fi prototypes all together.

Who

is my audience ?

The target audience is primarily designers working in visual design space. These folks nominally create multiple fidelities for designs. The tool would be helpful for them to allow click-through prototyping while comparing two designs or screens side-by-side.

Ideation

Ideation

The challenge was to create a web-based tool for aiding fellow designers and HCI students in my cohort at university. Just two weeks earlier to this, I was creating paper-prototypes for my very first classroom project. I was fascinated by the design evolution between iterations.
So, I thought of somehow combining the most enjoyable parts of projects. After hours of brainstorming, I finally decided build a collborative showcase tool for UX practitioners to visualize lo-fi and hi-fi resolution of designs they work with. These users can showcase their designs on a split-screen like display.
In addition, the tool would extend the current click-through capabilities for existing services like Invision.

Paper Prototyping

Paper Prototyping

I created paper prototypes after initial ideation to quick validate the project idea.
I started sketching out both the authoring and the previewing views. I chose to focus on having a coherent stack of controls which would employ ample affordance. And so, the drag-bar handler was quite effective for feedforward.

Feedback #1

For authoring view, move controls to a single pane, either to left or right, to minmise jumping around the screens.

Feedback #2

For previewing, look to enable both horizontal and vertical splits for comparing fidelities.

Feedback #3

Add mutliple screen and device masks to enhance preview experience.
