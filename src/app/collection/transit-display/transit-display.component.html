The assignment
We took into account papers which spoke of how people were influenced by virtual pets and how they serve as catalysts for behaviour change.

Persona #1

Elizabeth Duarte

Dictionary.com’s word of year for 2018 is “misinformation". We are trying to mitigate the dissonance created by the "fake" news around us. The propagation of such malicious content can be attributed to our own social circles.

Persona #2

George Shimko

The rampant spread of “fake” news is due to people seeking out and believing information which aligns with their currently-held attitudes. We blindly trust the source i.e. a friend of ours who re-tweeted or shared the article from somewhere (ELM).

Persona #3

Patrick Mulvaney

We are designing an avatar based mobile game to empower folks against such misinformation. As they progress through this embedded game design, they learn to look at content critically. We are employing multiple persuasive design strategies like fictionalization and obfuscation.

Hand Drawn Sketches

We created a fictional world in which users are given a creature, affectionately called a Worble, to take care of.
Throughout the course of the game, users receive information from friends in their pet community on how to take care of their Worble. The correct information will help their Worble grow whereas the malicious ones will make it sick.
Users accelerate their experience points as they correctly differentiate between the true and false information.

Final Prototype

After three iterations of testing our fidelities, we chose react-native for digital prototyping. The platform was new to us but offered a little bit of familiarity with its use of flex-based layouts. The richer native integrations, like using accelerometer for detecting a shake gesture, has been well-received.
