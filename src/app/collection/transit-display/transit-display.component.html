Transit Hub Display

Designing a data-driven display for a tourist hub

What

are we building ?

We are building an environmental display for the fictional Atlantis travel hub in the San Juan Islands. Passengers will get ample information to plan itineraries, take trips across the small islands and enjoy the peace and tranquility of the beautiful San Juan.

Tenet #1

People can plan itineraries to visit multiple islands and places of attractions on the island.

Tenet #2

We will promote the specific attractions and sight-seeing options across the different islands.

Tenet #3

The display would promote the PTF program through which passengers combine multiple trips across POIs in the islands.

Analyzing stakeholders

To dissect through the data, we studied the personas and the available transit information for the islands.
To plot the inferred information, we used Wurman's LATCH model and observed data across multiple features and axes.
We created multiple tables to see what factors were absolutlely vital and what information could be ommitted from views.

Persona #1

Elizabeth Duarte

Our Stakeholder serves as the transportation director of the municipality of the San Juan Islands. Her department is is funding the environmental display for showing transportation data across the island. She aims to promote the new program, PTF pass, which would allow customers great ease and flexibility to travel across the islands

Persona #2

George Shimko

He is a long-time resident of the island and owns modest fishing cabins on nearby Orcas Island and Stuart Island. He regularly invites his children and grandchildren to his house on San Juan Island and intends to give them PTF passes so that they can travel across San Juan Islands.

Persona #3

Patrick Mulvaney

Patrick Mulvaney is an environmental engineer stationed in Seattle. He spends an average of three days per week visiting drilling and wind power sites throughout the San Juan Islands. Weather often influences his travel and so Patrick welcomes the new Pass program that allows him to purchase multiple trips and optimize his travel on-the-fly.

Hand Drawn Sketches

After gathering and analyzing the data set and the user infomation, we moved on to hand drawn sketches. We did the 3-2-1 parallel sketching to get as many initial versions as possible.

Iteration #1

Moving to digital protoyping

Combining the best of the hand-drawn sketches till now, we decided to have an individual section for promoting the PTF pass. We focussed on visual coupling to highlight both trip data and map info. The highlighted part in the map keeps rotating to display the current island.

Critique #1

The lack of accuracy of the map took some credibility of the design away

Critique #2

Moving far away from the display, the subtle color hide a lot information

Iteration #2

Using motion as feedback

Based on the feedback, we went with a darker color palette, gave the map a more notable amount of space and worked on typographgy choices to enhance the text on the screen.

Motion Use #1

We added motion to show where the vehicle is relative to the origin and destinations.

Motion Use #2

Each card had a status that changes when the vehicle arrived, is ready to board or is boarding.

Motion Use #3

We wanted the card to disappear when the vehicle departs from the station bringing in the next trip.

Final Prototype

The final iteration displays the next departures per location. Users can find out more if they are interested, by interacting with a physical control (this has an up and a down arrow).
We used a card layout which reveals more information when interacted with. Also, the map highlights the routes related to the currently highlighted point of interest in the San Juan Islands.
