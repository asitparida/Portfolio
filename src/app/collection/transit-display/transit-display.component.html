Transit Hub Display

Designing a data driven display for a tourist hub

What

are we building ?

We are building a environmental display for the fictional Atlantis travel hub in the San Juan Islands. Passengers will get ample information to plan itineraries, take trips across the small islands and enjoy the peace and tranquility of the beautiful San Juan.

Tenet #1

People can plan itineraries to visit multiple islands and places of attractions in the island.

Tenet #2

We will promote the specific attractions and sight-seeing options across the different islands.

Tenet #3

The display would promote the PTF program through which passengers combine multiple trips across planes, trains and ferries operating across all airports, harbours and POIs in the islands.

Analyzing stakeholders

We studied the personas and the available transit information for the islands to dissect through the data.
To plot the inferred information, we used Wurman's LATCH model and observed data across multiple features and axes.
We created multiple tables to see what factors were absolutlely vital and what information could be ommitted from views.

Persona #1

Elizabeth Duarte

Our Stakeholder serves as the transportation director of the municipality of San Juan Islands which is financing the environmental display for showing transportation data across the island. She aims to promote the new program, PTF pass, which would allow customers great ease and flexibility to travel across the islands

Persona #2

George Shimko

He is a long-time resident of island and owns modest fishing cabins on nearby Orcas Island and Stuart Island. He regularly invites his children and grandchildren to his house on San Juan Island and intends to give them PTF passes so that they can travel across San Juan Islands.

Persona #3

Patrick Mulvaney

Patrick Mulvaney is an environmental engineer stationed in Seattle. He spends an average of three days per week visiting drilling and wind power sites throughout the San Juan Islands. Weather often influences his travel and so Patrick welcomes the new Pass program that allows him to purchase multiple trips and optimize his travel on-the-fly.

Using motion as feedback

We created a fictional world in which users are given a creature, affectionately called a Worble, to take care of.
Throughout the course of the game, users receive information from friends in their pet community on how to take care of their Worble. The correct information will help their Worble grow whereas the malicious ones will make it sick.
Users accelerate their experience points as they correctly differentiate between the true and false information.

Final Prototype

After three iterations of testing our fidelities, we chose react-native for digital prototyping. The platform was new to us but offered a little bit of familiarity with its use of flex-based layouts. The richer native integrations, like using accelerometer for detecting a shake gesture, has been well-received.
