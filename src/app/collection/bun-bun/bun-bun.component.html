Why

did I pick this project ?

This project was my first segway into paper-prototyping after I joined my master's program. The evolution of the design from lo-fi iterations to mid-fi and then building out the exact pixel perfect screens for a web-app was a fascinating experience. I could see the gradual improvement of the screens and understand why lo-fi protoypping and guerilla testing are essential during the ideation and discovery phases.

Visual Design

The first step was to create a visual language for the design. I looked at current phone and web apps for inspiration which did a pretty awesome job with in the food-commerce space. The Coolors site was an awesome tool to browse through some cool color palettes which related to cinnamon rolls and bakery in general.

Paper Prototyping

Iteration #1

I created low-fidelity paper prototypes to quickly test out the screens. The idea was to validate out the skeleton for the web-app. The online e-shopping experience was created using papers, post-its and sharpies. I also created simplied use-cases for baseline testing.

Use-Case #1

Alex is Business Person who knows what exactly she wants. She wants quick ordering and a custom order.

Use-Case #2

Pam is a Business Event Organizer. She is looking for good offers on catering. She also prefers to bulk order for a large event.

Guerilla Testing & Next Iterations

Feedback #1

Images inside the carousel in the homepage dont make sense and could be just changed to full bleed images.

Feedback #2

In the final confirmation page – the details after order is processed did not reflect the context earlier i.e. if store pick or delivery was selected.

Feedback #3

The call-to-action in the this iterations doesn't make sense as the ‘ORDER’ button is not easily discoverable.
