Why

did I pick this project ?

This project was my first segway into paper-prototyping after I joined my master's program. The evolution of the design from lo-fi iterations to mid-fi and then building out the exact pixel perfect screens for a web-app was a fascinating experience. I could clearly sense the gradual improvement of the screens and understand why lo-fi prototyping and guerilla testing are essential during the ideation and discovery phases.

Design Language

Visual Language

The first step was to create a visual language for the design. I looked at the existing phone and web apps, in the food-commerce space, for inspiration. The Coolors site was an excellent tool to browse through some cool color palettes related to cinnamon rolls and bakery in general.

Paper Prototyping

Paper Prototyping

Iteration #1

I created low-fidelity paper prototypes to test out the screens quickly. The idea was to validate out the skeleton for the web-app. The online e-shopping experience was created using papers, post-its, and sharpies. I also created simple use-cases for baseline testing.

Use-Case #1

Alex is a Business Person who knows what exactly she wants. She wants quick ordering and custom order.

Use-Case #2

Pam is a Business Event organizer. She is looking for reasonable offers on catering. She also prefers to bulk order for an event.

Testing

Guerilla Testing & Next Iterations

Feedback #1

In the homepage, images inside the carousel dont make sense and could be just changed to full bleed images.

Feedback #2

In the final confirmation page – the details after order is processed did not reflect the context earlier i.e. if store pick or delivery was selected.

Feedback #3

The call-to-action in the this iterations doesn't make sense as the ‘ORDER’ button is not easily discoverable.
